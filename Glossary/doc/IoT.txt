Internet of Things (IoT)
Network of connected devices that communicate and share data.

Sensor
Device that detects physical changes and converts them into signals.

Actuator
Device that converts electrical signals into physical motion.

Edge Computing
Computing done near the data source to reduce latency.

Smart Device
Electronic device that connects and interacts via the internet.

Connectivity
Ability of a device to connect and communicate over a network.

Big Data
Large volumes of data analyzed for insights.

Machine Learning
AI that allows systems to learn from data and improve.

Cloud Computing
Delivery of computing services over the internet.

M2M (Machine-to-Machine)
A broad label that can be used to describe any technology that enables networked devices to exchange information and perform actions without the manual assistance of humans.

Wearable Technology
Devices worn to collect and transmit data.

Smart Home
Home with ready for use devices for automation and control.

RFID (Radio-Frequency Identification)
Technology for wireless identification and tracking.

LoRaWAN (Long Range Wide Area Network)
Low power, long-range wireless protocol.

Smart City
Urban area using smart system to improve services and quality of life.

Gateway
A device that connects intelligent system devices to the cloud, providing communication and data transfer between sensors and cloud servers.

Telematics
The branch of information technology which deals with the long-distance transmission of computerized information. 

Smart Grid
An electricity supply network that uses digital communications technology to detect and react to local changes in usage, improving efficiency and reliability.
